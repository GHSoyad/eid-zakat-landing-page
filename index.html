What is Zakat in Islam?

Zakat is the third pillar of Islam. It signifies the wealth a Muslim pays to the poor and less privileged people.

Zakat is a compulsory act of worship and represents the responsibility and duty the richer have towards those in need. The word Zakat means both 'purification' and 'growth'.

Help Bring A Sustainable Social Change

As one of the pillars of Islam, Zakat is a form of obligatory charity that has the potential to ease the suffering of millions. Zakat is obligatory when a certain amount of money, called the Nisab, is reached or exceeded. Zakat is not mandatory if the amount owned is less than this Nisab.
